Our story

Built on an argument neither of us won.

Static & Stems started in a garage with one espresso machine, one turntable, and a disagreement about which deserved more patience.

In 2018, Kunal Verma was roasting coffee in small batches out of his garage, obsessing over roast curves the way some people obsess over spreadsheets. His neighbor, Meher Anand, ran a growing collection of jazz and Tamil film-score records and refused to let anyone play them on anything less than a proper turntable.

They kept arguing about which one required more patience to get right — a good roast or a good pressing. Neither backed down, so in 2019 they opened a single room in Bilaspur that did both: a roastery counter by day, a listening bar by night. Static & Stems has run out of the same building ever since, slowly adding a second sound system, a proper crate wall, and a back room for private sessions.

The rule hasn't changed since day one: whatever's on the platter plays start to finish. No skipping to the single, no talking over the bridge.

How we got here

A short, slow timeline

2018

The garage era

Kunal starts roasting small batches at home. Meher's record collection crosses 400 titles and takes over the living room.

2019

Static & Stems opens

One room, one counter, one turntable. Open four days a week to start, coffee only until 6pm.

2021

The listening nights begin

Weekend evening sessions added after regulars kept asking to stay past closing. Thursday becomes Resident Selector night.

2023

The back room opens

A second, smaller room is built for private bookings — birthdays, listening parties, the occasional very serious first date.

2026

1,400+ records, seven weekly sessions

Still the same two people behind the counter most nights. Still the same rule about playing the whole record.

What we won't compromise on

A few house rules

Whole records only

Once a side starts, it plays through. If you don't like a track, that's what side B is for.

No genre snobbery

Jazz, dub, ambient, 90s hip-hop, Tamil film scores — if it's pressed on wax and it's good, it earns a spot on the platter.

Small batches, always

We roast what we can sell in a week. No bag sits on the shelf long enough to go stale.
